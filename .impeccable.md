## Design Context

### Users

Buching is used by small teams, schools, studios, nonprofits, service businesses, training academies, community groups, and workshop organizers that run scheduled events, classes, programs, sessions, or services. Core users are operators: owners, admins, managers, and staff who need to create events, manage attendees and registrations, coordinate resources, accept payments, publish booking pages, and monitor day-to-day booking activity. Public users are attendees who need to quickly browse trustworthy event pages and reserve a spot, especially on mobile.

### Brand Personality

Calm, operational, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a capable business workspace for running real operations, not a playful consumer app or a glossy marketing surface. It should create confidence, clarity, and control for organizers, while public booking flows should feel polished, reliable, and easy to complete.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained enterprise workspace aesthetic built on shadcn-style primitives, Base UI, Tailwind tokens, Inter for body text, and Instrument Sans for heading treatment. The app uses neutral surfaces, a warm orange primary color, subtle borders, light fills, comfortable radius, and soft shadows for elevated work surfaces, dialogs, popovers, cards, and active states. Dashboard screens should lean on sidebar navigation, tables, kanban lanes, calendars, operational panels, and dense scannable layouts instead of generic card mosaics. Public booking pages can be more polished and approachable, but should remain trustworthy and organization-branded. Support both light and dark themes through existing tokens.

Avoid generic GPT/shadcn card grids, loud gradients, ornamental color, overly sharp corners, heavy outlines, saturated panels, and default SaaS dashboard cliches. Do not use color as decoration by default; reserve stronger color for status, feedback, and primary actions.

### Design Principles

- Lead with operational clarity over marketing gloss, especially inside the dashboard.
- Use typography, spacing, and layout structure before borders or decoration to create hierarchy.
- Prefer table, timeline, calendar, kanban, and panel layouts when they communicate operational state better than cards.
- Keep routine workspace UI calm; reserve stronger color and elevation for status, feedback, focus, and important actions.
- Make public booking and registration flows polished, mobile-first, trustworthy, and fast to complete.
- Preserve the established design system: neutral tokens, warm primary, comfortable radius, soft shadows, shadcn/Base UI primitives, and sidebar-first navigation.
